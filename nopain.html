If You’re Thinking About Ending Your Life, Please Pause for a Moment

If you’re here, it means something important: a part of you is still reaching for relief, not disappearance.

You may feel exhausted beyond words. You may have tried to endure, to understand, to explain, to hold on—and nothing seems to ease the pain anymore.

If your thoughts are saying, “I can’t do this anymore,” we want you to know this first:

You are not weak.
You are overwhelmed by pain that has lasted too long.

Something Important You Deserve to Hear

Wanting to end your life does not mean your life has no value. It means the pain you are carrying has become heavier than what one person can bear alone.

When suffering is intense and prolonged, the mind narrows. It begins to believe that stopping life is the only way to stop pain.

This is not a personal failure. It is a human response to unbearable distress.

You Do Not Have to Decide “Forever” Right Now

Please don’t force yourself to answer questions like:

  • Should I live or die?
  • Does my life have meaning?

Those questions are too heavy for this moment.

Instead, we gently ask just this:

Please don’t make an irreversible decision right now.
Give yourself time. Just stay alive for this moment.

Not for the future. Not for anyone else. Just to let this wave of pain pass a little.

If You Feel Like You’re at the Edge

Try saying this to yourself, even quietly:

  • I don’t have to carry this alone.
  • I need support, not answers.
  • I can stay alive today, and think later.

If you can, tell one real person what you’re going through—a friend, a family member, a volunteer, a counselor, anyone.

Pain spoken aloud loses some of its power.

If You Feel You Might Hurt Yourself Soon

Please read this carefully:

If you believe you may act on these thoughts, or you have a plan to harm yourself, please seek immediate help right now.

  • Contact your local emergency services
  • Or reach out to a suicide crisis line
  • In the United States: Call or text 988 (Suicide & Crisis Lifeline)
  • In other countries: Please contact your local emergency number or a crisis hotline in your area

Asking for help is not a burden. It is an act of self-preservation.

We Want to Stay With You a Little Longer

You may not believe things will get better—and that’s okay. We are not asking you to believe anything right now.

Only this:

Your pain is real.

You deserve to be understood.

And you do not have to go through this alone.

If you can, please remain here in this world a little longer. Even just for today. Even just for this hour.

The fact that you are still here matters.

If You’re Willing to Take One More Step

  • Reach out to someone you trust
  • Contact a mental health or crisis professional
  • Stay here, breathe, and allow support to find you

You do not need to be okay. You only need to not be alone.

We are here.
