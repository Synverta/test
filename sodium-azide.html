Overview

Sodium Azide is an inorganic azide commonly used in laboratory settings as a preservative and as a reagent in synthesis and analytical workflows. It is highly toxic; avoid contact and inhalation and follow all applicable safety and waste-disposal requirements.

Handling & Safety

  • Use appropriate PPE: lab coat, chemical-resistant gloves, and eye protection.
  • Avoid generating dust; handle in a well-ventilated area or fume hood as appropriate.
  • Keep away from acids and incompatible materials; consult SDS for incompatibilities.
  • Dispose of waste in accordance with local regulations; do not pour into drains.
  • Not for ingestion, inhalation, or any use outside qualified laboratory research.
